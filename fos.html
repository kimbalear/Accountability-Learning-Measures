Services and service subcategories
 
Contraceptive Services
 
Contraceptives – Oral Contraceptives – Consultation ℹ
A trained service provider sees a person and gives comprehensive information about oral contraceptives either for the first time or as a follow up to ensure that medical eligibility criteria are met, monitor for side-effects or resupply the person, in a space which allows confidentiality. When a person receives a consultation service, it should be recorded as a service provided, even if the person does not adopt this contraceptive method.

Emergency Contraception (EC) – Counseling ℹ
A trained counselor/service provider shares person-specific non-directive advice about all emergency contraceptive (EC) methods following an act of unprotected sex to ensure the person fully understands their emergency contraceptive options and the method failure rates, in a space which allows confidentiality. This counseling is separate from contraceptive general counseling because of the distinct nature of the counseling content: for example in relation to sexual violence and negotiation skills of safe sex; consideration of STI exposure and the need for contraceptive general counseling to encourage the person to take up a modern method of contraception.
 
 
 
 
 
Emergency Contraception (EC) – Consultation ℹ
A trained service provider sees a person and gives comprehensive information about all emergency contraceptive (EC) methods following an act of unprotected sex to ensure that medical eligibility criteria are met, monitor for side-effects and minimize the chances of an unplanned pregnancy, in a space which allows confidentiality. (Clients should receive EC – counseling as well as an EC consultation. When a person receives a consultation service, it should be recorded as a service provided, even if the person does not adopt this contraceptive method.

Abortion Services
Abortion – Counseling – Pre-abortion/Options Counseling ℹ
When a person has an unplanned pregnancy, a trained counselor/service provider provides person-specific non-directive advice about pregnancy options (abortion, continuing pregnancy and adoption); discussion of available abortion methods suitable to the person (risks and benefits); post-abortion contraceptive options; and identification of other sexual and reproductive health needs. This service ensures that the person is aware of all options available to them if they decide to have an abortion, and that informed consent is obtained, in a space which allows confidentiality. This counseling should be reported regardless of which of the options the person chooses, including the decision not to have an abortion. This may also be referred to as ‘Options counseling’.
 
 
 
 
 
Abortion – Counseling – Post-abortion ℹ
A trained counselor/service provider provides person-specific advice and guidance about after-care and psycho-social support (if needed) following an induced abortion or treatment of incomplete abortion (provided by the partner organization or outside) to ensure the person has no remaining questions or concerns and to determine whether further psyco-social support is required, in a space which allows confidentiality. (Contraceptive counseling should also be given and reported under ‘Contraceptives – Counseling – General’).

People reached with SRH services refers to the total number of unique individuals that received one or more SRH services from your organization in the last year. A person is counted only once, even if they had multiple visits where they received numerous different services during the same year.
This total can be generated by using patient identifiers (such as calculating the total number of unique patient record numbers or ID numbers in your clinic management information system for the last year), or it can be estimated.
If your organization does not provide SRH services to people, skip to the next tab.

Comprehensive Sexuality Education (CSE) refers to a curriculum-based process of teaching and learning about the cognitive, emotional, physical, and social aspects of sexuality. It aims to equip young people with knowledge, skills, attitudes, and values they need to determine and enjoy their sexuality.
  • Curriculum: A written, evidence-based guide for teaching and learning that includes key objectives, as well as structured guidance for the delivery of clear content and skills.
There are 4 types of CSE sessions that are conducted with young people:
  • Peer Educator Training: Initial training or ongoing supportive training to young people as peer educators or as youth health promoters who facilitate sexuality education sessions.
  • Complete Series: Engaging young people (who are not training to be peer educators) in a complete set of multiple sexuality education sessions that cover standard content areas. A complete series covers topics such as sexual rights, gender, sexuality, interpersonal relationships, communication and decision-making, and sexual and reproductive health, among others.
  • Partial series: Engaging young people in a series of sexuality education sessions that does not cover all standard content areas of your organization’s CSE curriculum.
  • Single Sessions: Engaging young people in one sexuality education session that covers a portion of content from your CSE curriculum.
